Restaurants Near Defiance Wine Country, MO

The lakeside dinner you didn't know was waiting at the end of your wine trail day.

Defiance, Missouri is the gateway to one of the best stretches of wine country in the Midwest. Chandler Hill, Sugar Creek, Yellow Farmhouse, Montelle, Augusta — a string of family-owned wineries that pulls thousands of visitors out from St. Louis every weekend. The Katy Trail runs right through. The Daniel Boone Home is around the corner. And by mid-afternoon, every visitor on the trail is asking the same question: where do we actually have dinner out here?

The Quarry is the answer most locals give. We're a 26-acre lakeside restaurant, wine bar, and live music venue just 12 minutes east of Defiance on Highway Z. Chef-crafted American food, a wine list that complements (rather than competes with) what you tasted at the wineries, an outdoor turf and patio area on the water, indoor golf simulator bays for rainy days, and live music most Friday and Saturday nights. We were built to be the dinner anchor for everyone who's been on the wine trail since noon.

Why The Quarry is the 12-minute drive worth making

Here's the situation if you're planning a Defiance day-trip: most of the wineries close at 5 or 6 p.m. The tasting rooms are amazing, but they're not built for a full dinner — most have light food, charcuterie boards, or seasonal grill menus, but the moment the sun starts dropping you need somewhere to land for a real meal. Driving back into St. Charles or downtown St. Louis kills the vibe of the day. Driving to Wentzville drops you back into chain country.

Twelve minutes east of Defiance gets you to The Quarry instead. You leave the last winery, follow Highway Z, and arrive at a property that feels like a continuation of the wine country experience, not a return to the suburbs. Our wine list was built specifically with Missouri vineyards in mind — a lot of the producers you tasted earlier in the day are on our list. Our chef takes the same locally-sourced approach the best winery kitchens use. And the lake outside is the kind of place you actually want to linger over dinner.

If you're hosting friends from out of town for a wine country day, planning a wedding weekend that includes a winery rehearsal dinner, or just wrapping up a Saturday on the Katy Trail, this is the dinner you're looking for.

How to find us from Defiance Wine Country

From Chandler Hill Vineyards or Sugar Creek Winery: head east on Highway 94 to Highway F, then connect to Highway Z east. About 12 minutes, 6 miles.

From Yellow Farmhouse Vineyard or Montelle Winery in Augusta: take Highway 94 east, then connect via Highway F to Highway Z. Roughly 18 minutes — a slightly longer but very scenic drive through the rolling Missouri countryside.

From the Katy Trail trailhead in Defiance: take Highway F north, then Highway Z east. About 12 minutes. This is the shortest and easiest route from the trail itself.

The drive from any Defiance winery is pure country road — rolling hills, vineyards, and the kind of two-lane scenery that makes the trip part of the experience.

What you can do at The Quarry on any given visit

Most people who arrive at The Quarry from a Defiance wine trail day are looking for a real dinner and a chance to slow down. We can absolutely do that — but there's also more here than just the meal if you want to make an evening of it:

  • Wood-fired American dinner — steaks, fresh seafood, and a kitchen that builds its menu around what's in season locally. Not pretentious, just well-made food worth slowing down for.
  • Curated wine bar — Missouri producers, small-batch regional wines, and a sommelier-built list that's designed to complement (not compete with) what you tasted at the wineries earlier.
  • Live music most Friday and Saturday nights — the perfect anchor for the back end of a wine country day.
  • Outdoor turf and patio overlooking the lake — fire pit, lakeside seating, and warm-weather evening dining that extends the wine-country feel.
  • Indoor golf simulator bays — when Missouri weather turns on you, six private golf bays let the day keep going. Perfect for groups of friends finishing a wine tour.
  • Wedding venue and event spaces — if you're planning a wedding in wine country, our lakeside venue is 12 minutes from every winery on the trail. Many of our couples build a full weekend around a winery rehearsal dinner and a Quarry ceremony. More on our weddings page.

If you're already planning a wine trail day, the smartest move is to reserve a table for the back end of your day in advance — Saturdays especially fill up fast. Our events calendar will tell you whether there's live music or a special event on the date you're planning.
