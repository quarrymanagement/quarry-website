Restaurants Near Wentzville, MO

Skip the strip-mall lineup. The Quarry is 15 minutes west — and worth every one of them.

If you live or work in Wentzville, you already know the dining scene there: it's mostly chains, mostly built around Wentzville Parkway and the I-70 corridor, and after a while it all starts to feel the same. The good news is that one of the most distinctive restaurants in St. Charles County is sitting just 15 minutes west of you on Highway Z — and most Wentzville locals still haven't found it.

The Quarry is a 26-acre lakeside restaurant, wine bar, and live music venue tucked into New Melle, Missouri. We're a chef-driven kitchen serving wood-fired American cuisine, an extensive wine list, six private indoor golf simulator bays for year-round play, an outdoor turf-and-patio area on the water, and a full event venue that hosts everything from rehearsal dinners to weddings to live music nights every weekend. None of that exists inside the Wentzville city limits, and the drive from town is genuinely shorter than people expect.

Why The Quarry is the 15-minute drive worth making

We didn't build The Quarry to compete with the chains in Wentzville. We built it to be the place you go instead of the chains — when you want a real dinner with someone who matters, when friends from out of town are visiting and you want them to remember the meal, when there's actually something to celebrate. That's the niche we filled, and it's why people who try us once tend to come back.

The kitchen is run by a chef who actually cares about where the food comes from. The wine list is built by people who taste everything before it goes on the menu. The live music is booked, not just hired. The wedding coordinator is full-time staff, not a vendor we share with five other venues. And the property itself — the 26 acres, the lake, the outdoor space — gives the experience a kind of atmosphere you can't manufacture in a strip mall.

If you're tired of the same six choices in Wentzville and you've already tried the obvious ones in O'Fallon, this is the next move.

How to find us from Wentzville

From downtown Wentzville and the Wentzville Parkway corridor, take Highway Z west. You'll cross under I-70, then continue through the rolling country roads past Pointe at Wentzville and out toward New Melle. The drive is roughly 8 miles and takes about 15 minutes in normal traffic — closer to 12 if you're coming from the south side of Wentzville near Timberland High School or Holt High School.

From the Wabash Park side of town, take Highway Z west and stay on it the entire way. It's a straight shot — no highway transitions, no toll roads, no complicated turns. Once you see the lake on your right, you've arrived.

What you can do at The Quarry on any given visit

One of the things people don't expect about The Quarry is how much actually happens here on any given week. It's not just dinner. Depending on which night you come out from Wentzville, you might find:

  • Friday or Saturday night live music — local and regional acts, with a kitchen running full menu service throughout.
  • Themed bingo and music trivia nights — including 80s Neon Bingo, ladies' brunch bingo, and seasonal fundraiser events.
  • Indoor golf simulator bays — bookable by the hour, perfect for date nights, corporate outings, or just escaping a Wentzville winter when nothing else is open.
  • Sunday brunch with live music — bottomless options and a relaxed lakeside vibe.
  • Private event spaces for birthdays, rehearsal dinners, corporate gatherings, and milestone celebrations — no minimum size, fully customizable menus.
  • The wedding venue — if you're planning a wedding or know someone who is, this is one of the most-toured venues in St. Charles County for a reason. Tours are by appointment.

The full events calendar lives on our events page, and reservations for any night of the week are at our reservations page. If you're planning a private party or larger group, private events lives here.

Reservations: Friday and Saturday nights book up early, especially when there's live music or a ticketed event on the calendar. We strongly recommend reserving a table in advance — walk-ins are welcome but seating is first-come, first-served once the floor fills up.
